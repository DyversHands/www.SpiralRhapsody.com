These are personal field notes — opinionated, specific, and incomplete by design. They document particular instruments I have played, owned, or studied closely, together with the sound models those instruments realize.

A sound model is the scale template: its interval structure, modal character, cultural lineage, and acoustic identity. The Pygmy scale is a sound model. But two instruments built on the Pygmy scale — even by the same maker, from the same steel — will not sound the same. Shell diameter, construction method, tuning approach, the age of the steel, the size of individual tone fields, the shape of the Gu port: all of it matters. What you hear is always a specific realization of the model, not the model itself.

These notes try to document both layers: the model and the instrument that embodies it. Where the two diverge — where a physical instrument departs from or exceeds the expectations its sound model sets — that divergence is the most interesting thing to record.

F Pygmy Variant
F Minor · Bb Minor ensemble · Pygmy Variant
Meditative and harmonically open — the Variant's three modal homes give it a restlessness the Classic doesn't have. The D2 Helmholtz sits foreign to the scale, adding a dark undertow that doesn't resolve. The upper register shimmers near Bb4.
PANArt Hang · 2004 · Sabina Schärer · 1st Generation · Serial 2814
G Pygmy
G Minor · C Minor ensemble · Pygmy Classic
One of the original instruments; hand-sunk, signed. Warm and ceramic, with an Eb2 Helmholtz that reinforces the scale's own ♭6. The Gu bell tone is intentionally oval-shaped by the makers — audible, musical, and characteristic of early PANArt craft. The upper register shimmers; the mid-register is grounded and clear.
Pantheon Steel Halo · August 2009 · Kyle Cox · Genesis · Serial 14
C XXD (Xiao Xiong Diao)
C Minor · F Minor ensemble · Xiao Xiong Diao
Baritone register, rolled steel, unusual coherence across the full range. The C2 Helmholtz sits on the root — the cavity reinforces rather than unsettles. Six notes instead of five give it more melodic vocabulary than the Pygmy instruments. Serial #14 is cited as an early masterwork.
Ensemble — C Natural Minor
C · D · E♭ · F · G · A♭ · B♭
All three instruments together cover every note of C Natural Minor with no gaps and no clashes. Each occupies a different structural position: the XXD anchors the root, the Panart voices Dorian from the 5th, and the Makai visits as a modal voice. The Pygmy Classic's ensemble formula and the Pygmy Variant's solo formula are identical — they mirror each other across the ensemble/solo boundary.
